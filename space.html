Space is the Place: To Our Solar System and Beyond!

From sunshine to the moon and the stars.. space is out there! Space exploration began in 1957 when the Soviet Union (present day: Russia) launched the first artificial satellite, Sputnik 1, into space. Years later, in 1969, astronaut Neil Armstrong made the "one giant leap for mankind" as he stepped onto the Moon. Since then, the exploration of space has continued--from infamous movies and tv-shows (yes, we're talking about Star Wars and Wall-E) to pop star Katy Perry making a trip in 2025!

Let's do our own kind of exploration on our Solar System and the galaxies that make up our Milky Way.

Our Solar System and its Planets

Planet Information Fun Fact!
Sun The Sun is a hot, glowing ball of gas and it keeps our Earth (and us) warm! The Sun is a star and appears much closer to us than the stars in the night sky (that's why it looks larger!). However, the Sun is just considered an average sized star. The Sun contains about 99% of ALL the material in our solar system!
Mercury Mercury is the smallest and one of the hottest planets in our solar system. It doesn't have any moons and is the closest planet to the Sun. Since it moves quite slowly, it takes 59 Earth days to make up 1 day on Mercury. Mercury has been known since the ancient times.
Venus Venus is full of greenhouse gases that traps heat and keeps Venus warm. It's considered the hottest planet in the Solar System (sorry Mercury) and can even make metals melt to a pool. Venus has volcanoes!
Earth We live on Earth! Earth's atmosphere has the right amount of thickness with mostly nitrogen to make us able to breathe! Earth is the only planet to harbor life and liquid water.
Mars Mars is a cold, cold planet (running minus 85 degrees Fahrenheit). The atmosphere is thin and it is a terrestrial planet. Mars has five rovers: Perseverance, Curiosity, Spirit and Opportunity, and Sojourner.
Jupiter Jupiter is the biggest planet in our solar system! And...it's a gas giant! Jupiter has 95 recognized moons.
Saturn Saturn is not the only planet with rings, but certainly has some of the most notable ones! The rings are made of chunks of ice and rock, but the planet itself is made of hydrogen and helium. The rings on Saturn act as a shield that contains destroyed moons and comets--oh wow!
Uranus Uranus is made of water, methane, and ammonia fluids above a rocky center--the atmosphere contains hydrogen and helium which creates its gorgeous blue color. Uranus rotates on its side and has a set of 13 rings!
Neptune Neptune is dark, cold, and very windy and has a thick, windy atmosphere. Neptune was discovered in 1846!

What about the galaxy?

Now that we learned about our solar system--what about beyond that? Well, our solar system is a small part of the Milky Way Galaxy. A galaxy is a huge collection of gas, dust, and tons of stars (that contain their own solar system)! Scientists believe that there could be as many as one hundred billion galaxies in the universe--wow! Let's talk more about the different types of galaxies that

  • Spiral galaxies: These galaxies look like giant pinwheels--our Milky Way is part of this group. These galaxies contain plenty of gas and dust are typically brighter than other galaxies as they contain newer stars (and newer stars burn brighter than older stars). They make up 60% of galaxies.
  • Elliptical galaxies: These galaxies are shaped like stretched-out ovals! These galaxies tend to contain older stars and contain very little dust and gas. They make up about 20% of galaxies.
  • Irregular galaxies: Unlike the other galaxies, they don't have a common shape and are (typically) the smallest galaxies that scientists have observed. Yet, they can be bright and contain plenty of young stars. Irregular galaxies make up 20% of galaxies.
  • Quasars: These galaxies are compact and contain a ton of energy and are super bright! Our Milky Way doesn't contain these types.

UtS's Galaxy Brownies

It's time to chef it up! Now that we learned a bit about space--let's bring it to the kitchen with these yummy galaxy inspired brownies.

Bring in a parent or trusted guardian to help you with these steps.

Ingredients

  • 10 pitted Medjool dates
  • 1/2 cup almond butter (OR 1/2 cup sunflower seed butter)
  • 5 tablespoons cacao powder
  • 1 tablespoon ground flax seeds (OR 1 egg)
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 teaspoon balsamic vinegar
  • one pinch of salt
  • pastel confetti sprinkles (or yellow sprinkles) :)

Let's bake!

  1. Preheat the oven to 350ºF and grease a sheet pan.
  2. In a food processor, combine the dates and water. Process until the dates have broken into a paste.
  3. Add the rest of the ingredients until the dough becomes uniform.
  4. Press the dough into the pan--make sure to wet your fingers as it can be sticky!
  5. Once the dough is in the pan, spread the sprinkles all over!
  6. Bake until the tops look dry (appx. 12-15 min.).
  7. Wait until cool, then serve!
  8. Enjoy with friends and family :D
