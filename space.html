Space is the Place: To Our Solar System and Beyond!

From sunshine to the moon and the stars.. space is out there! Space exploration began in 1957 when the Soviet Union (present day: Russia) launched the first artifical satellite, Sputnik 1, into space. Years later, in 1969, astronaut Neil Armstrong made the "one giant leap for mankind" as he stepped onto the Moon. Since then, the exploration of space has continued--from to pop star Katy Perry making a trip!

Planet Information Fun fact!
Sun Mercury Venus Earth Mars Jupiter Saturn Uranus Neptune
The Sun is a hot, glowing ball of gas and it keeps our Earth (and us) warm! The Sun is a star and appears much closer to us than the stars in the night sky (that's why it looks larger!). However, the Sun is just considered an average sized star. Mercury is the smallest and one of the hottest planets in our solar system. It doesn't have any moons and is the closest planet to the Sun. Since it moves quite slowly, it takes 59 Earth days to make up 1 day on Mercury. Venus is full of greenhouse gases that traps heat and keeps Venus warm. It's considered the hottest planet in the Solar System (sorry Mercury) and can even make metals melt to a pool. We live on Earth! Earth's atmopshere has the right amount of thickness with mostly nitrogen to make us able to breathe! Mars is a cold, cold planet (running minus 85 degrees Fahrenheit). The atmosphere is thin atmosphere and is a terrestrial planet. Jupiter is the biggest planet in our soalr system! And...it's a gas giant! Saturn is not the only planet with rings, but certaintly has some of the most notable ones! The rings are made of chunks of ice and rock, but the planet itself is made of hydrogen and helium. Uranus is made of water, methane, and ammonia fluids above a rocky center--the atmosphere contains hydrogen and helium which creates its gorgeous blue color. Neptune is dark, cold, and very windy and has a thick, windy atmosphere.
The Sun contains about 99% of ALL the material in our solar system! Mercury has been known since the ancient times. Venus has volcanoes! Earth is the only planet to harbor life and liquid water. Mars has five rovers: Perseverance, Curiosity, Spirit and Opportunity, and Sojourner. Jupiter has 95 recognized moons. The rings on Saturn act as a shield that containe destroyed moons and comets--oh wow! Uranus rotates on its side and has a set of 13 rings! Neptune was discovered in 1846!
