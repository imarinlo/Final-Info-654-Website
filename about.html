About This Site

Down to Space was created by four graduate students in the Library and Information Science and Information Experience Design programs at Pratt Institute. We all come from different backgrounds and interests, but we share one thing: a love of learning and a belief that science should be exciting and accessible for everyone, especially kids. This site grew out of a simple observation: children ask incredible questions about the world around them, but the answers are often too complicated or scattered across too many places. We wanted to fix that.

We organized the site around the idea of layers, from deep underground all the way up to outer space, because we think that's how science actually works. Everything is connected. A fossil underground tells us about ancient climates. Soil feeds the food on our plates. The sky brings the rain that waters the plants. And the sun, a star in space, drives it all. We hope this site helps kids see those connections and leaves them curious to learn more.

Meet the Team

Minnie Marin Lopez

Minnie has a background in education and design, and is passionate about making information beautiful and easy to understand. She was drawn to this project because she wanted to create something she would have loved as a curious kid who always had too many questions. Minnie wrote the Homepage, the Soil and Food page, and this About page. She also built the JavaScript food search component on the Soil page, which uses the Open Food Facts API to let kids look up real nutritional information for any food they can think of.

Sarah-Ann Jean Paul

Sarah-Ann has always been fascinated by weather and the natural world. Growing up, she was the kid who ran outside during thunderstorms instead of away from them. She wanted to work on this project because she believes that understanding weather and the seasons helps kids feel more connected to the world around them. Sarah-Ann wrote the Sky page, covering clouds, the water cycle, and the four seasons. She also built the JavaScript weather component on the Sky page and integrated the weather APIs.

Manvi Shah

Manvi has a deep love of natural history and paleontology. She grew up visiting museums and was always the one dragging everyone to the dinosaur exhibits. She joined this project because she wanted to bring that same sense of wonder to kids who might not have easy access to those experiences in person. Manvi wrote the Underground page, which covers dinosaurs, fossils, and the fossilization process. She drew from the Smithsonian and the American Museum of Natural History to make sure the content was accurate and engaging.

Daria Shulga

Daria is passionate about space exploration and loves following NASA missions. She believes that space is one of the most powerful ways to spark curiosity in young people, and that now is an especially exciting time with missions like Artemis bringing renewed attention to what lies beyond our planet. Daria wrote the Space page and built the interactive JavaScript component that lets kids explore planets and learn fun facts about each one.

Why Children's Science?

We chose to focus on younger audiences because we believe that the questions kids ask, "Why is the sky blue?", "How did dinosaurs become fossils?", "Where does rain come from?", deserve real, thoughtful answers that don't talk down to them. Kids are natural scientists. They observe, they question, and they experiment. Our goal was to meet them where they are and give them a resource that respects their curiosity.

We also wanted to create something useful for parents and teachers who are trying to answer those questions in the moment and need a reliable, kid-friendly place to point to.
